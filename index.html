Welcome.

You have to respect your employees. Not only does everyone deserve proper treatment regarding their ethnicity, gender and personality, but also a proper compensation when an employee shows potential, hard work and loyalty providing benefits to their workspace.

This is a playful take on the unfair terms some of us had or could experience as an employee. Either being a minimum wage worker for a retail store, a coworker working at a restaurant on a tip based salary, or as a freelancer dealing with the absurdity of their clients.

Scenario: John D. [Your Friendly Manager]

Setting: A Regular Retail Store

Despite employees' rights, some employers and higher ups find gray zones/ loopholes that allow them to lower someone’s worth without getting in trouble with the law. Using manipulation, gas lighting, threats and guilt tripping actions into loyalty of their workers. And not everyone can afford losing their position as it’s their only source of income. Some examples can be seen with waiters/waitresses, retail stores, minimum-wage workers, part-time students, intern stages, newly employed, or even those who had been working in the same environment for a long period of time.

Scenario: Missy C. [Young Coworker]

Setting: Working At A Restaurant

Colleagues can choose to combine tips. One can also opt to share them with other employees in the workplace. However, the employer cannot force an employee to share tips with others or set up a voluntary tipping pool. If a tip-sharing plan is established, it must be mutually agreed upon by all workers involved. Yet sometimes, employers do find a way to turn that option into a rule without making it feel wrong.

Scenario: Dweet F. [A customer you'll probably see once]

Setting: Dealing with clients as a Designer

Dealing with clients can become troublesome. Many don’t understand the work designers put into their fictional work. Sometimes, their minds go out of bounds, and it's their job to either meet their requirements to achieve clients’ satisfaction or push them towards a simpler solution that also works. The important part is to stand their ground and charge extra for extra time they need to ‘fix’ the layout based on clients suggestions. Their demands can become absurd, it's a designer’s job to make their dream design realistic based on the budget they offer.
